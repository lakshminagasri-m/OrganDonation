What is Organ Donation?

Organ donation is the process of surgically removing an organ or tissue from one person and transplanting it into another person. This selfless act can save or improve the lives of up to 8 people, and enhance the lives of many more through tissue donation.

Why is Organ Donation Important?

There are currently over 100,000 people waiting for an organ transplant in the United States alone. Unfortunately, many of these individuals will not receive a transplant in time, and will die waiting. By becoming an organ donor, you can help reduce the shortage of available organs and give hope to those in need.

How Do I Become an Organ Donor?

To become an organ donor, you can: - Register online through your state's donor registry - Sign up when you apply for or renew your driver's license - Tell your family and friends about your decision to become a donor
